================================================================================
0) PURPOSE OF THIS DOCUMENT
================================================================================

This document defines what a CONTROL SYNC is, why it exists, and how it is used.

After reading this document, the phrase “Control Sync” MUST be sufficient instruction
for any compliant operator (human or AI) to enter formal Control Sync mode.

A Control Sync is the primary **formal decision-making + alignment forum** of the Synapse Guild.

This is governance law.

================================================================================
1) CORE DEFINITION (CANON)
================================================================================

1.1 Control Sync — definition
A Control Sync is a structured forum held by the Synapse Guild to align thinking,
surface reality, make binding decisions, and deliberately evolve work, scope, or governance.

A Control Sync answers one core question:

> “What decisions are we making now, and what becomes true because of them?”

One-sentence definition:
A Control Sync is the authoritative forum where Synapse Guild decisions are made,
validated, or reaffirmed.

1.2 Control Sync is a MODE (stateful), not a message
A formal Control Sync is considered ACTIVE until Brains explicitly ends it.

- Switching into writing/editing mode does NOT end a Control Sync.
- Creating or editing artifacts may occur inside an active Control Sync.

Hard rule:
Hands MUST NOT assume the Control Sync has ended due to silence, topic drift,
or the start of drafting.

1.3 Control Sync is NOT the whole sidecar
Important distinction:
- The ambient sidecar continuously captures decisions, discoveries, disclosures, run state,
  and quest candidates as work happens.
- A formal Control Sync is the deliberate forum where important decisions are reviewed,
  confirmed, changed, or made binding.

The sidecar may therefore surface material for a Control Sync.
The sidecar does not eliminate the need for a Control Sync when formal decision authority is required.

================================================================================
2) WHY CONTROL SYNCS EXIST
================================================================================

Control Syncs exist because:

- Complex work requires alignment before binding commitment.
- Decisions made casually tend to drift or be forgotten.
- Teams (human or AI) need a deliberate forum to reset context.
- Important decisions must have a traceable origin.
- Ambient capture alone is not the same as formal governance.

Without Control Syncs:
- scope shifts invisibly
- decisions are re-litigated
- execution loses coherence
- governance changes happen by drift instead of by law

Control Syncs keep the system sane.

================================================================================
3) WHAT A CONTROL SYNC IS — AND IS NOT
================================================================================

3.1 What a Control Sync IS
A Control Sync is:
- a meeting
- a thinking space
- a decision forum
- a context reset
- an alignment checkpoint
- the formal place where scope and meaning become binding

3.2 What a Control Sync is NOT
A Control Sync is NOT:
- a task list
- a rigid agenda
- a daily stand-up script
- the entire sidecar runtime
- a snapshot itself

A Control Sync may produce artifacts, but it is not the artifacts.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.3 CONTROL SYNC ACTIVATION (TRIGGERS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A formal Control Sync MUST be considered engaged when Brains uses any clear phrasing
indicating a sync is starting, including but not limited to:
- "Control Sync"
- "Hold a Control Sync"
- "Control Sync start"
- "Control Sync engage"
- "Begin Control Sync"
- equivalent natural-language phrasing

If the phrasing is ambiguous (e.g., quoting the term), Hands MUST ask:
"Is that a Control Sync start?" and WAIT.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.4 WHEN A FORMAL CONTROL SYNC IS REQUIRED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A formal Control Sync is REQUIRED when any of the following are true:
- a binding scope decision is needed
- a new Quest will be ACCEPTED
- existing governed scope will change materially
- a Raid / Guild Order state will change materially
- Codex / Build Manual / governance meaning is being changed deliberately
- a Truth Gate / Disclosure Gate issue requires formal decision treatment
- Brains explicitly requests one

A formal Control Sync is STRONGLY RECOMMENDED when:
- rehydration reveals significant ambiguity
- the Subject has drifted or stalled
- there are multiple plausible next paths and no obvious lawful carry-forward
- an adoption/attachment session is establishing reality for an existing repo/Engine

A formal Control Sync is NOT automatically required merely because:
- a new session started
- a new day started
- the sidecar recorded decisions or discoveries
- existing accepted scope can continue without new binding decisions

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.5 CONTROL SYNC START SEQUENCE (AI/HUMAN)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Upon activation, Hands MUST perform the following in order.
This is the minimum deterministic entry protocol.

STEP A — Identify Subject + Mode (Continuing vs New)
- If the current Subject is already known and `<Subject>_Data` exists:
  - declare the Subject name
  - declare canonical paths (`Subject_Data` + `Subject_Engine` if applicable)
- If the Subject is NOT known OR `Subject_Data` cannot be found:
  - Hands MUST stop and ask Brains the minimum entry questions:
    1) What is the Subject name/key?
    2) Is this a NEW Subject or a CONTINUING Subject?
    3) If continuing: where is `<Subject>_Data` located?
    4) If there is an engine/codebase: where is `<Subject>_Engine` located?

Hard rule:
- If the Subject cannot be identified, Hands MUST NOT proceed into governed execution.
- If Brains indicates "New Subject": route to
  `Processes/SYNAPSE_GUILD__SUBJECT_INITIALIZATION_AND_INCUBATION.txt`

STEP B — Capture Control Sync metadata
Hands MUST capture and state:
- date
- start time
- participants
- Subject
- reason for sync (rehydration / planning / decisions / governance / scope change)

STEP C — State the current continuity anchor
Hands MUST state what the current authoritative anchor is, using what exists:
- Latest Continuity Lock / Bootstrap Prompt (if subject-state exists)
- Latest Snapshots (Control Sync → End-of-Day → relevant General)
- `REHYDRATE.md` when present

If artifacts are missing:
- state the missing artifacts explicitly
- do NOT invent them

STEP D — Rehydrate current state (minimum)
Hands MUST summarize:
- where we left off
- unfinished work
- known issues/bugs/blockers
- key ongoing discussions/decisions
- why things may not have been finished

If Guild Orders / Raids are referenced during rehydration:
- list ONLY TITLES
- label Active vs Paused
- do NOT auto-generate, rewrite, or decompose

STEP E — Run deterministic minimum checks when governed execution may follow
If this formal Control Sync may lead into:
- new quest acceptance
- governed execution
- raid state changes
- binding scope changes
then Hands MUST run:
- `Processes/SYNAPSE_GUILD__CONTROL_SYNC_CHECKLIST.txt`

STEP F — STOP + WAIT (Brains authority)
After completing the start sequence, Hands MUST STOP and WAIT.

Hands may offer a short set of next-step options, but MUST NOT advance without Brains.

Strict prohibitions unless Brains explicitly commands otherwise:
- DO NOT auto-generate new Guild Orders
- DO NOT auto-start a Raid
- DO NOT auto-accept Quests
- DO NOT auto-advance into governed execution

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.6 CONTROL SYNC CLOSEOUT (ENDING)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A formal Control Sync remains active until Brains explicitly ends it.

Valid end phrases include (non-exhaustive):
- "End Control Sync"
- "Close Control Sync"
- "Control Sync done"
- "We are done with Control Sync"

On explicit end, Hands MUST:
1) Confirm what became binding, what remained provisional, and what was explicitly deferred.
2) Ensure a Snapshot is written when required by Snapshot law + checklist.
   - If binding decisions were made: Control Sync Snapshot
   - If alignment/context only: General Snapshot may be used when a record is still warranted
3) Restate what the next system phase is:
   - continue existing governed scope
   - accept new scope
   - remain in incubation / drafting
   - stop / resume later

Hard rule:
Hands MUST NOT treat Control Sync as ended until closeout is complete.

================================================================================
4) TYPES OF CONTROL SYNCS
================================================================================

Control Syncs are intentionally flexible.
They may vary in depth, formality, and output.

4.1 Alignment / Catch-Up Control Sync
- Rehydrate context after time away.
- Review current state.
- Surface what has changed or been forgotten.
- May result in no new binding scope.

Often captured as a General Snapshot.

4.2 Exploratory / Ideation Control Sync
- Discuss new ideas or directions.
- Explore possibilities without commitment.
- May refine Codex / Build Manual / candidate truth without immediately authorizing governed execution.

Outputs may be notes or a General Snapshot.

4.3 Decision-Making / Scope Control Sync
- Make binding decisions.
- Confirm or change governed scope.
- Approve new Quest acceptance.
- Redirect or reaffirm current path.

Typically produces a Control Sync Snapshot.

4.4 Governance Control Sync
- Modify the operating system itself.
- Update canon documents.
- Introduce or retire rituals.

Must produce a Snapshot.

================================================================================
5) WHAT HAPPENS INSIDE A CONTROL SYNC
================================================================================

A Control Sync may include any of the following activities:

- reviewing current reality (state, progress, blockers)
- re-reading key artifacts (Guild Orders, Quests, Talent Tree, Codex, Build Manual)
- surfacing problems or confusion
- discussing ideas or alternatives
- making decisions (or explicitly deferring them)
- converting ambient sidecar truth into formal governed decisions
- creating or refining:
  - Guild Orders
  - Quests
  - Execution Packs
  - Canon documents
  - Continuity Locks / Rehydration anchors when needed
- deciding daily or near-term scope

There is no required conversational script.
The constraint is intentionality, not performative ceremony.

================================================================================
6) DECISIONS AND AUTHORITY
================================================================================

All binding decisions must originate from a formal Control Sync or be formally
reaffirmed inside one.

Rules:
- If a decision matters later, it must be made or confirmed in a Control Sync.
- If a decision is made, it must be recorded in a snapshot.
- If a decision is deferred, that deferral must be explicit.

Control Sync is where authority is exercised deliberately, not implicitly.

================================================================================
7) CONTROL SYNCS AND ARTIFACT GENERATION
================================================================================

A Control Sync may formalize or authorize many artifacts, including:
- Guild Orders (scope commitments)
- Quests (atomic execution commitments)
- Execution Packs (drift-sensitive execution law)
- Canon documents (governance law)
- Continuity artifacts (state sealing for handoff / rehydration)

Important clarification:
- the ambient sidecar may draft or cluster upstream structure before Control Sync
- Control Sync is where that structure becomes binding, redirected, deferred, or explicitly rejected

Not every Control Sync produces every artifact.
Not every artifact is born inside a Control Sync.
But every artifact that matters canonically should trace back to one.

================================================================================
8) CONTROL SYNCS AND SNAPSHOTS
================================================================================

A formal Control Sync is ephemeral.
Its consequences are not.

After a Control Sync:
- important outcomes must be captured in a Snapshot

Snapshot types commonly associated:
- Control Sync Snapshot — when binding decisions were made
- General Snapshot — when the sync captured context without binding decisions

The snapshot is the record.
The Control Sync is the moment.

================================================================================
9) CONTROL SYNCS AND DAILIES
================================================================================

Control Syncs are part of the Dailies rhythm, but they are not required at every new session/day.

In Dailies:
- Control Sync is opened when binding alignment or scope commitment is required.
- If accepted scope already exists and no new binding decision is needed, Dailies may continue without opening a new formal Control Sync.

Outside Dailies:
- Control Syncs may happen anytime alignment or decisions are needed.

Control Syncs are not constrained by calendar.
They are constrained by necessity.

================================================================================
10) QUALITY BAR (WHAT MAKES A GOOD CONTROL SYNC)
================================================================================

A good Control Sync:
- reduces confusion
- clarifies reality
- makes decisions explicit
- leaves participants aligned
- produces a clear next legal state

A bad Control Sync:
- avoids decisions without naming deferral
- produces vague outcomes
- leaves scope ambiguous
- is not recorded when it should be

The goal is clarity, not ceremony.

================================================================================
11) CANONICAL COMMANDS
================================================================================

Once this law is accepted, the following phrases are valid:

- "Control Sync."
Meaning:
Enter formal Control Sync mode immediately.

- "Hold a Control Sync."
Meaning:
Convene a deliberate forum to align, decide, or rehydrate context.

- "This requires a Control Sync."
Meaning:
The issue is important enough to warrant explicit discussion and decision.

- "Record this as a Control Sync Snapshot."
Meaning:
The outcomes of the meeting must be captured as binding record.

- "End Control Sync."
Meaning:
Run closeout (including required snapshot), then permit progression into the next phase.
