================================================================================
0) PURPOSE OF THIS DOCUMENT
================================================================================

This document defines what QUESTS are, why they exist, and how they are used inside
the Synapse Guild Operating System.

After reading this document, the phrase “Create a Quest” MUST be sufficient
instruction for any compliant operator (human or AI).

This requires deterministic handoff:
- A Quest MUST be executable by an operator who did not write it.
- If a Quest relies on implicit context, it is invalid and MUST NOT be ACCEPTED.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
QUEST vs SIDE-QUEST (NAMING + ORIGIN)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Definitions:
- QUEST_### : A quest explicitly derived from an ACTIVE Guild Orders document (part of the current Raid plan).
- SIDE-QUEST_### : A quest created ad-hoc during work sessions or Control Syncs to address discovered bugs,
  unblock progress, or capture non-planned work.

Rules:
- QUEST and SIDE-QUEST follow the same lifecycle and evidence rules:
  BOARD → ACCEPTED → COMPLETED / ABANDONED, with audits required for completion.
- SIDE-QUEST MUST be clearly labeled in BOTH:
  - filename prefix: "SIDE-QUEST_"
  - quest ID inside the quest document: "SIDE-QUEST_###"
- SIDE-QUEST MUST NOT be treated as fulfilling Guild Orders unless explicitly promoted.

Promotion (SIDE-QUEST → Guild Orders scope):
- Promotion is a binding decision.
- Promotion MUST be recorded during a Control Sync and captured in the Control Sync Snapshot.
- If the Guild Orders document is amended to include the work, the SIDE-QUEST may remain a SIDE-QUEST;
  "promotion" refers to scope attribution, not renaming.

Authority note (BUFFS):
- Subject Buffs may specify subject-specific execution surfaces, tooling, and receipt conventions.
- Subject Buffs MUST NOT override Governance law.
- If a Buff conflicts with this document, this document wins.

Quests are the atomic execution unit of the Synapse Guild.
They are where intent becomes real work.

This is governance law.

================================================================================
1) CORE DEFINITION (CANON)
================================================================================

1.1 Quest — definition
A Quest is a single, clearly scoped, executable commitment that can be completed,
verified, and recorded independently.

A Quest answers one question:

> “What is the next concrete thing we are committing to do?”

One-sentence definition:
A Quest is an atomic unit of committed work with explicit scope and completion criteria.

1.2 Atomicity test (deterministic)
A Quest is atomic if and only if ALL are true:
- It has exactly one primary objective.
- Its completion criteria can be verified independently.
- Its verification does not require completing a second independent objective.

If a proposed "Quest" requires multiple independently verifiable outcomes,
then it is NOT a Quest.
It MUST be decomposed (usually into multiple Quests under one Dungeon).

================================================================================
2) WHAT PROBLEM QUESTS SOLVE
================================================================================

Quests exist because:

- Large objectives must be broken into finishable pieces.
- “We should do X” is not actionable without commitment.
- Execution without clear boundaries leads to sprawl and half-finished work.
- Progress must be auditable, not implied.

Without Quests:
- work remains vague
- tasks overlap
- completion is subjective
- talent progression becomes meaningless

Quests convert intent into accountability.

================================================================================
3) WHAT QUESTS ARE — AND ARE NOT
================================================================================

3.1 What Quests ARE
Quests are:
- atomic commitments
- execution-scoped
- independently completable
- auditable after the fact

3.2 What Quests are NOT
Quests are NOT:
- ideas
- brainstorming notes
- vague to-do items
- multi-week mega-projects
- Guild Orders
- Dungeons
- Execution Packs

If something cannot be finished and verified on its own, it is not a Quest yet.

================================================================================
4) SOURCES OF QUESTS (WHERE THEY COME FROM)
================================================================================

Quests may originate from multiple places.

4.1 From Guild Orders (most common)
- Guild Orders define Dungeons.
- Each Dungeon MUST be decomposed into one or more Quests.
- Completing all relevant Quests satisfies the Dungeon.

4.2 From Maintenance or Technical Debt
- Refactors
- Cleanup work
- Structural fixes
These may exist outside active Guild Orders.

4.3 From Bugs or Incidents
- A defect discovered during execution
- A correctness violation
- A broken invariant

4.4 From Governance or Meta-System Work
- Updating documentation
- Renaming or reorganizing artifacts
- Improving the operating system itself

Not all Quests are born from Guild Orders.
That is intentional.

5) QUEST STATES (CANON)
================================================================================

Quests move through explicit states represented by their location on the Quest Board.

State law:
- Quest state is defined by WHERE the Quest file lives.
- Metadata inside the file does not override location.

Canonical locations (see: Quest Board/SYNAPSE_GUILD__QUEST_BOARD.txt):
- BOARD:
  <Subject>_Data/Quest Board/
- ACCEPTED:
  <Subject>_Data/Quest Board/Accepted/
- COMPLETED:
  <Subject>_Data/Quest Board/Completed/
- ABANDONED:
  <Subject>_Data/Quest Board/Abandoned/

Rules:
- A Quest becomes committed work only when it is ACCEPTED.
- ACCEPTED implies intent to execute.
- Work MUST NOT begin on a Quest that is not ACCEPTED.

World State Gate (execution legality):
- Under Fog of War, Quests may be drafted on BOARD, but MUST NOT be ACCEPTED.
- BOARD → ACCEPTED validation FAILS if Codex Freeze marker does not exist:
  <Subject>_Data/Codex/CODEX_FREEZE.md

See:
- Quest Board/SYNAPSE_GUILD__QUEST_VALIDATION_RULES.txt
- The Guild/Terminology/Locks/LOCK__FOG_OF_WAR.md
- The Guild/Terminology/Locks/LOCK__CODEX_FREEZE_FOG_LIFTED.md

================================================================================
6) QUEST CREATION (WHEN AND HOW)
================================================================================

6.1 When to create a Quest
Create a Quest when:
- a concrete task has been identified
- scope can be clearly defined
- completion criteria can be stated
- the work can be verified independently

Do NOT create a Quest when:
- the work is still vague
- decisions are unresolved
- scope cannot be bounded

In that case, keep it as a note or discuss it in a Control Sync first.

6.2 How Quests are created
- Quests MUST be created using the canonical Quest Template.
- One Quest = one file.
- The Quest file lives on the Quest Board in BOARD state until accepted.

See:
- Quest Board/QUEST_TEMPLATE.txt
- Quest Board/SYNAPSE_GUILD__QUEST_VALIDATION_RULES.txt

================================================================================
7) QUEST EXECUTION (HOW WORK HAPPENS)
================================================================================

Quests are executed through the Dailies loop.

High-level flow (summary):
1) Control Sync — alignment + decide scope (which Quests to accept and/or execute today)
2) Accept Quest(s) — move valid Quests to ACCEPTED
3) Implement — execute accepted Quests (Quest Mini-Loop)
4) System Verification — test/verify the system as appropriate for the Subject
5) Snapshot — record what changed and why
6) Stop / Resume

The Dailies document is authoritative for the full daily execution topology.
See:
- Processes/SYNAPSE_GUILD__DAILIES.txt

Quests do not dictate *how* work is done.
They define *what must be true* when work is finished.

================================================
7.1 EXECUTION AUDITS (COMPLETION REQUIREMENT)
================================================

A Quest is not considered COMPLETED unless its execution is provable.

Synapse OS enforces this via Execution Audits.

Canonical rule:
- Every executed Quest MUST have the mandatory Execution Audit phases:
  1) Pre-Quest Alignment Audit
  2) Execution Audit
  3) Verification Audit
  4) Post-Quest Outcome Note

Storage law:
- Execution Audits live under:
  <Subject>_Data/Audits/Execution/
- Canonical structure is an audit bundle folder per quest:
  <Subject>_Data/Audits/Execution/QUEST_###__YYYY-MM-DD__<short_slug>/

Canonical bundle minimum (required files):
- 00_SUMMARY.md   (MANDATORY, human-readable)
- 01_PREQUEST.md  (MANDATORY)
- 02_EXECUTION.md (MANDATORY)
- 03_VERIFY.md    (MANDATORY)
- 04_OUTCOME.md   (MANDATORY)

Quest file requirement:
- Each ACCEPTED Quest file MUST record its audit bundle folder path
  (see Quest Board/QUEST_TEMPLATE.txt).

Verification law (anti-hallucination):
- The Verification Audit MUST record what was actually checked.
- Simulated runs are invalid ("reasoned PASS" is not PASS).
- If the execution surface cannot perform verification, the Quest MUST NOT be marked COMPLETED.

Multi-day / retries:
- The audit bundle remains canonical.
- Append attempt blocks inside the existing audit phase files.

Completion rule:
- Marking a Quest COMPLETED without these audits is an invalid state transition.

See:
- Processes/SYNAPSE_GUILD__EXECUTION_AUDITS.txt

================================================================================
8) DEPENDENCIES AND RELATIONSHIPS
================================================================================

Quests may depend on other Quests.

Rules:
- Dependencies MUST be explicit in the Quest file.
- Implicit ordering is forbidden.

Execution rule:
- A Quest MUST NOT be executed unless its dependencies are satisfied.

Acceptance rule:
- A Quest may be ACCEPTED with unsatisfied dependencies ONLY if it is intentionally staged:
  - dependencies are listed
  - and the plan states how/when the dependency will be satisfied

Multiple Quests may relate to the same Dungeon, system, or capability.
That does not make them hierarchical.

================================================================================
9) TALENT POINTS AND QUESTS
================================================================================

Not all Quests award Talent Points.

9.1 Talent-awarding Quests
A Quest awards a Talent Point only if it:
- introduces a new capability, OR
- expands an existing capability in a meaningful way

Examples:
- adding a new pipeline stage
- enabling search
- adding a new interface door

9.2 Non-talent Quests
Quests that typically do NOT award Talent Points:
- bug fixes
- refactors
- cleanup
- documentation updates
- renaming/reorganizing files
- maintenance tasks

Law:
- The Quest MUST explicitly state whether it awards a Talent Point.
- No automatic assumption is allowed.

If capability changed:
- update the Talent Tree
- record the update in the Quest Outcome and the End-of-Day Snapshot

================================================================================
10) QUESTS VS GUILD ORDERS (CRITICAL DISTINCTION)
================================================================================

Guild Orders define scope and intent.
Quests define execution commitments.

Guild Orders:
- answer “what are we building in this phase?”
- are few and stable

Quests:
- answer “what are we doing next?”
- are many and transient

A Quest may exist without Guild Orders.
Guild Orders are executed through Quests.

================================================================================
11) QUALITY BAR (WHAT MAKES A GOOD QUEST)
================================================================================

A good Quest:
- has clear scope
- has explicit out-of-scope
- has objective completion criteria
- can be completed without redefining itself
- can be executed by an operator who did not write it

A bad Quest:
- mixes multiple independent objectives
- has fuzzy boundaries
- relies on implicit knowledge
- cannot be verified when finished

Quality gate:
- If a Quest is vague or non-executable, it MUST NOT be ACCEPTED.
- Fix the Quest (or decompose it into smaller Quests) first.

================================================================================
12) CANONICAL COMMANDS
================================================================================

Once this law is accepted, the following phrases are valid:

- “Create a Quest.”
Meaning:
Author a new atomic work commitment using the Quest Template and place it in BOARD state.

- “Accept this Quest.”
Meaning:
Validate the Quest, create/record its audit bundle path, and move it to ACCEPTED.
(If Codex Freeze marker does not exist, acceptance is invalid.)

- “Complete this Quest.”
Meaning:
Completion criteria are satisfied AND the audit bundle exists with PASS verification.
Then move the quest file to COMPLETED and record the result in the End-of-Day Snapshot.

- “Abandon this Quest.”
Meaning:
Intentionally drop the commitment; record why (Control Sync + Snapshot as required),
then move the quest file to ABANDONED.
