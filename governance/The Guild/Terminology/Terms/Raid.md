# TERM — Raid

## Definition
A Raid is the governed execution state of a Guild Orders artifact.

A Raid is defined by artifact state (state by location):
- **ACTIVE** Raid: a valid Guild Orders file exists in `<Subject>_Data/Guild Orders/ACTIVE/`
- **PAUSED** Raid: the Orders exist in `<Subject>_Data/Guild Orders/PAUSED/`
- **COMPLETED** Raid: the Orders exist in `<Subject>_Data/Guild Orders/COMPLETED/`

Conversation does not start, pause, or complete a Raid. Artifact state does.

## Core meaning
A Raid is not “work in general.”
A Raid is the moment a declared body of scope becomes official governed execution.

This means a Raid is:
- post-drafting
- post-alignment
- tied to canonical Guild Orders
- subject to Quest acceptance, audits, verification, and snapshots

## Purpose
- Bind governed execution to declared scope (Guild Orders) and prevent drift
- Coordinate Dungeons → Quests toward an explicit definition of DONE
- Provide a stable execution target across multiple sessions (Dailies)
- Distinguish official governed work from ambient exploration, drafting, or candidate formation

## When It Applies
A Raid applies only when ALL are true:
- World State law permits governed execution (Fog Lifted)
- a valid Guild Orders artifact exists for the current build scope
- Brains has started the Raid by moving the Orders into ACTIVE

Drafting or revising Guild Orders in PAUSED is not active raid execution.
It is raid preparation.

## What It Enables
A Raid enables:
- governed execution against a declared scope boundary
- Dungeon grouping under one scope artifact
- Quest acceptance and execution under that scope
- auditable progress tracking (Execution Audits + Snapshots)
- session continuity around active or paused governed work

## What It Does NOT Enable
A Raid does NOT:
- override the Codex
- authorize silent scope changes
- make BOARD quests automatically executable
- make Quests valid or complete by itself (Quest law still applies)
- exempt Risk / Consent Gates, Truth Gate, or Disclosure Gate
- convert ambient exploratory work into governed work automatically
- absorb SIDE-QUESTS into Guild Orders scope unless they are explicitly promoted

## Relationship to Fog of War
Fog of War does not forbid drafting Guild Orders.
Fog of War forbids starting a Raid.

Therefore:
- Guild Orders may exist in PAUSED while Fog is active.
- Dungeons may be drafted.
- BOARD quests may be drafted.
- The Raid itself remains illegal until Fog is lifted.

## Relationship to Dailies
Dailies are the governed human rhythm of an active or continuing Subject.
A Raid supplies the current scope boundary inside that rhythm.

Dailies may:
- continue an existing Raid
- pause a Raid
- complete a Raid
- review whether a new Raid should be started

But Dailies do not create legality on their own.
World State + artifact state do.

## Multiple Raids
Multiple Raids may exist at once.
Multiple Raids may also be ACTIVE at once.
When more than one Raid is ACTIVE, Control Sync must declare which Raid or Raids are in scope for the session.

## Interactions
- Raids execute Guild Orders.
- Raids contain Dungeons.
- Dungeons decompose into Quests.
- Quests are executed through Dailies once ACCEPTED.
- SIDE-QUESTS may occur during a Raid, but they do not satisfy Guild Orders scope unless explicitly promoted.

## Do Not Assume
- A Raid is not a plan.
- A Raid is not a document section.
- A Raid is not “all work currently happening.”
- A Raid is not the same as ambient quest detection.
- A Raid is not allowed merely because work exists.
- A Raid begins only when the canonical artifact state says it begins.
