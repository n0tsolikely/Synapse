# TERM — Control Sync

## Definition
A Control Sync is a structured alignment session used to rehydrate state, review continuity, make decisions, and set direction.

A Control Sync persists until explicitly ended by the authority role.

## Purpose
Control Sync prevents drift by ensuring shared understanding of current state, scope, and intent before work proceeds.

## When It Applies
- At the start of work sessions (rehydration)
- When resuming after a pause
- When changing direction or scope
- During both Fog of War and Execution phases

## What It Enables
- Review of prior Control Sync snapshots
- Review of End-of-Day execution outcomes
- Decision-making and prioritization
- Scope declaration for upcoming work

## Do Not Assume
- A Control Sync is not execution
- A Control Sync does not automatically generate new Guild Orders/Quests unless directed
- A Control Sync does not end on its own

## Interactions
- Ending a Control Sync produces a Control Sync Snapshot
- Control Sync decisions constrain Guild Orders, Quests, and Dailies
