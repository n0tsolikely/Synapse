# TERM — Codex (Map)

## Definition
The Codex is the authoritative map of a Subject: what exists, how it is structured, what is allowed, and what is forbidden.

**Alias (conversation):** The Codex may be referred to as the **Map**.

Artifact law:
- The Codex is a Subject-state artifact and lives in `<Subject>_Data/Codex/`.
- Conversation is input. The Codex artifact is authority.

World State gate:
- Governed execution is legal only after Codex Freeze.
- Codex Freeze is proven only by the existence of:
  - `<Subject>_Data/Codex/CODEX_FREEZE.md`

## Purpose
The Codex eliminates ambiguity around what the Subject IS.
It preserves governing truth so the Subject can be resumed, rebuilt, or reasoned about without guesswork.

## When It Applies
- During Incubation / Fog of War: the TOC and Codex may be drafted and expanded.
- During ambient sidecar operation: Codex growth may continue while the Subject is still being discovered.
- After Codex Freeze (Fog Lifted): the Codex constrains Guild Orders, Dungeons, accepted Quests, and governed execution.
- During ongoing work: Codex changes remain deliberate changes recorded through Control Sync and related continuity artifacts.

## What It Enables
- Deterministic scope selection via Guild Orders
- Deterministic decomposition (Guild Orders → Dungeons → Quests)
- Shared meaning anchors (definitions + invariants)
- Stable reference points for Vision Alignment and execution legality

## Codex maturity
Useful working language while the Subject is still under Fog:
- Codex Seed
- Codex Draft
- Codex Spine
- Codex Frozen

Freeze means the Codex spine is stable enough for governed execution.
It does not mean every possible explanatory detail is permanently complete.

## Do Not Assume
- The Codex is not execution and is not a backlog.
- The Codex is not the Build Manual.
- The Codex is not the Talent Tree.
- A draft TOC or draft Codex is not execution authority.
- The Codex does not change implicitly.
  - If it is not written into the Codex artifact, it is not a binding Codex rule.
- A concise Vision surface may exist, but it does not replace the Codex.

## Interactions
- The TOC (Legend) is the navigational contract for the Codex and provides the canonical section numbering.
- Guild Orders, Dungeons, and Quests MUST reference Codex sections by ID (TOC numbering) where applicable.
- Codex Freeze transitions World State: Fog of War → Fog Lifted.
- If work cannot be traced back to a Codex section (or an explicitly deferred decision), it is not execution-ready.
