================================================================================
0) PURPOSE
================================================================================
Synapse Governance is universal because it operates on SUBJECTS, not software.
This document defines the minimal Subject model used by the Guild.

================================================================================
1) DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

1.1 SUBJECT
A SUBJECT is any bounded body of work that evolves over time.
Examples (non-exhaustive): Ashby, Stuart, a novel, a game, an essay, a website, homework.

1.2 <Subject>_ENGINE
The Engine is the thing itself (the product).
- Code: the source repo / app
- Book: the manuscript text (chapters)
- Game: game source + scripts/levels
- Essay: the draft document

1.3 <Subject>_DATA
The Data bundle is supporting continuity + state for the subject.
It can contain: snapshots, continuity locks, research/notes, decisions, guild orders,

Subject_Data skeleton lock:
- The minimum required directory skeleton for <Subject>_Data is locked.
- See: The Guild/Terminology/Locks/LOCK__SUBJECT_DATA_SKELETON.md
quests, logs, references, lore bibles, etc.

1.4 CODEX (PER SUBJECT)
Codex is constitutional law for the subject:
- invariants
- definitions
- constraints
- non-goals
- architectural/creative rules that must not drift

================================================================================
2) UNIVERSAL DIRECTORY RULES
================================================================================

2.1 Governance stays universal
Synapse Governance (this folder) defines universal rules, lifecycles, naming law, and rituals.

It MUST NOT hard-code a subject’s unique internal structure.
However, it MAY define a universal minimum Subject_Data skeleton that applies to all subjects.

Canonical reference:
- Processes/SYNAPSE_GUILD__SUBJECT_INITIALIZATION_AND_INCUBATION.txt

2.2 Subject-specific trees live in <Subject>_Data
Each subject may add its own internal structure inside its own Data bundle,
on top of the universal skeleton.

Recommended location (inside the subject bundle):
- <Subject>_Data/Blueprint/Subject_Structure.md  (or equivalent)

2.3 Examples are allowed, defaults are not
Ashby/Stuart may be referenced as examples, but no rule may assume those subjects.
